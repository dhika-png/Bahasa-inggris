📖 Narrative Text

What is Narrative Text?

A narrative text is a type of text that tells a story to entertain or amuse the reader. It usually has a clear plot with a beginning, middle, and end. Narrative texts can be fiction or based on real events, and they often include characters, settings, and a sequence of events.

Generic Structure

  1. Orientation — Introduces the characters, setting, and time of the story.
  2. Complication — A problem or conflict arises that the main character must face.
  3. Resolution — The problem is solved, either happily or sadly.
  4. Re-orientation (optional) — A closing remark or moral lesson.

Language Features

  • Uses past tense (e.g., lived, went, said)
  • Uses adverbs of time (e.g., once upon a time, long ago)
  • Uses action verbs (e.g., ran, fought, climbed)
  • Uses direct speech (e.g., "I will find you," she said)
  • Uses conjunctions of time (e.g., then, after that, finally)

Types of Narrative Text

  • Fairy Tales — Stories with magical elements
  • Fables — Stories with animal characters and moral lessons
  • Legends — Stories about the origin of a place
  • Myths — Traditional stories about gods
  • Folktales — Stories passed down through generations
"The purpose of narrative text is to entertain the reader with a story that has a clear plot, interesting characters, and often a moral lesson."

📌 Example:

"Once upon a time, in a small village nestled between two mountains, there lived a young girl named Lina. She was known throughout the village for her kindness and bravery."
