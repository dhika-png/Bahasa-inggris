Text Type

📝 Recount Text

What is Recount Text?

A recount text retells events or experiences that happened in the past. Its purpose is to inform or entertain by describing what happened, when it happened, and who was involved. Unlike narrative text, recount text does not have a complication or conflict.

Generic Structure

  1. Orientation — Background information: who, when, where.
  2. Events — A series of events in chronological order.
  3. Re-orientation — Closing statement or personal comment.

Language Features

  • Uses simple past tense (e.g., went, visited, saw)
  • Uses chronological connectors (e.g., first, then, next, finally)
  • Uses action verbs (e.g., walked, ate, played)
  • Uses specific participants (e.g., I, my family)
  • Uses adverbs of time and place (e.g., last week, at the beach)

Types of Recount Text

  • Personal Recount — The writer's own experience
  • Factual Recount — Records events factually
  • Imaginative Recount — Retells events from an imaginative perspective
"Recount text tells the reader what happened by listing events in the order they occurred. There is no conflict — just a retelling of past experiences."

📌 Example:

"Last holiday, my family and I went to Bali for a week. We stayed at a hotel near Kuta Beach and planned to visit several tourist attractions."
