Text Type 01

Narrative Text

Narrative text is a type of text that tells a story to entertain, inspire, or convey a moral lesson. It involves characters, a setting, and a sequence of events that build toward a climax and resolution.

The main purpose of narrative text is to amuse or entertain the reader while sometimes delivering an implicit message or lesson. It is one of the most common text types found in literature, films, and everyday storytelling.

🏗️ Generic Structure

  • Orientation — Who, when, where
  • Complication — Problem arises
  • Resolution — Problem solved
  • Reorientation — Optional ending

✏️ Language Features

  • Past tense verbs
  • Time connectives (then, after, suddenly)
  • Descriptive adjectives
  • Direct & indirect speech
  • Action & mental verbs
PartFunctionExample Opening
OrientationIntroduces characters & setting"Once upon a time, in a small village…"
ComplicationPresents the central conflict"One day, a fierce dragon appeared…"
ResolutionResolves the conflict"Together, they found a way to defeat it…"
ReorientationClosing moral or reflection"From that day on, the village lived in peace."

📖 Short Example

The Lost Key

Orientation: One cold winter evening, a young girl named Lila was walking home through the forest.

Complication: Suddenly, she realised she had lost the key to her house. The wind was howling and darkness fell quickly. She searched everywhere but could not find it.

Resolution: Just as she was about to give up, a friendly fox appeared and led her back to the path where the key had fallen. With a grateful heart, Lila ran home safely.

Reorientation: She never forgot that kindness can come from the most unexpected places.

The Brave Little Turtle


Once upon a time, in a peaceful lake surrounded by tall trees, there lived a small turtle named Timo. Unlike the other turtles who were content to stay close to the shore, Timo always dreamed of exploring the deep end of the lake.


One day, Timo finally decided to swim toward the deep water. However, halfway through, a strong current pulled him far from his home. He struggled against the waves but could not find his way back. The other turtles watched from the shore, too afraid to help.


Just when Timo was exhausted and about to give up, an old wise fish named Coral appeared. "Follow the direction of the reeds," she said calmly. Timo trusted her advice and slowly swam in that direction. After a long struggle, he finally reached the shore safely.


From that day on, Timo became the most respected turtle in the lake, and he always reminded others that courage, combined with wisdom, can overcome any obstacle.

Text Type 02

Recount Text

Recount text retells past events or experiences in the order they happened. Unlike narrative text, it does not necessarily involve a conflict or complication — the main goal is simply to inform or entertain by revisiting what occurred.

Recount texts can be personal (diary entries, holiday stories), factual (historical events, news reports), or imaginative. The language is typically clear, chronological, and written in the past tense.

🏗️ Generic Structure

  • Orientation — Who, when, where, why
  • Events — Sequence of what happened
  • Reorientation — Closing thought or reflection

✏️ Language Features

  • Simple past tense
  • Time connectives (first, then, next, finally)
  • First-person (I, we) for personal recount
  • Third-person for factual recount
  • Specific participants
PartFunctionExample
OrientationSets the scene"Last Saturday, my family and I visited Bromo Volcano."
Event 1First thing that happened"We woke up at 3 AM and started the hike."
Event 2Next event in sequence"At sunrise, we reached the viewpoint."
ReorientationPersonal reflection"It was an unforgettable experience."

📖 Short Example

My Trip to Bromo

Orientation: Last month, my family and I decided to visit Mount Bromo in East Java. We were very excited as it was our first trip there.

Events: We left home at midnight and arrived at the parking area at 3 AM. First, we rode a jeep through the sea of sand. Then, we climbed the stairs to the crater's edge. The smoke and the smell of sulfur made the experience truly dramatic.

Reorientation: Watching the sunrise over the volcanic landscape was breathtaking. I will never forget that magical morning as long as I live.

My First Day at a New School


Last Monday was my first day at SMP Nusantara, a school I had just transferred to after my family moved to a new city. I felt very nervous that morning as I put on my new uniform.


First, my mother drove me to school. When I arrived, the security guard greeted me kindly and showed me to my classroom. My homeroom teacher, Mr. Hendra, introduced me to the class in front of everyone, which made my face turn red.


During the break, a girl named Sari approached me and invited me to eat lunch with her group of friends. They were very welcoming and funny. We talked about our favourite subjects and hobbies. I found out that two of them also loved playing badminton, just like me.


By the end of the day, I no longer felt like a stranger. I went home smiling, grateful that my first day had turned out so much better than I had expected.

Text Type 03

Short Biography

A short biography is a brief written account of a real person's life. It highlights the key facts, achievements, and contributions that made the person notable. The text is factual, informative, and usually written in the third person.

Short biographies are commonly found in encyclopedias, school textbooks, and official profiles. They aim to inform the reader about who the person is, what they accomplished, and why they are remembered.

🏗️ Generic Structure

  • Introduction — Who the person is
  • Life Events — Childhood, education, career
  • Achievements — Notable contributions
  • Conclusion — Legacy or summary

✏️ Language Features

  • Third-person pronouns (he, she, they)
  • Simple past tense
  • Factual & descriptive language
  • Dates and specific details
  • Action verbs (founded, won, inspired)
PartFunctionExample
IntroductionIdentify the subject"R.A. Kartini was an Indonesian national hero."
Life EventsKey moments of their life"She was born in Jepara on April 21, 1879."
AchievementsWhat they accomplished"She pioneered education for Indonesian women."
ConclusionLasting legacy"Her struggle is commemorated every April 21st."

📖 Short Example

R.A. Kartini

Introduction: Raden Ajeng Kartini was one of Indonesia's most celebrated national heroes, widely known for her advocacy for women's rights and education.

Life Events: She was born on April 21, 1879, in Jepara, Central Java. As a Javanese noblewoman, she had access to Dutch education, which was rare for women at the time. Through letters to Dutch friends, she expressed her progressive ideas about equality and freedom.

Achievements: Kartini established a school for Javanese women in 1903, breaking social barriers of her era. Her letters were later compiled into a famous book, Door Duisternis tot Licht (From Darkness to Light).

Conclusion: Kartini passed away in 1904, but her legacy lives on. Her birthday, April 21st, is celebrated as Kartini Day throughout Indonesia, honoring her lasting contribution to women's empowerment.

Habibie — The Father of Indonesian Technology


Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie was born on June 25, 1936, in Parepare, South Sulawesi. He was one of Indonesia's most brilliant minds and is widely regarded as the father of Indonesian aviation technology.


At a young age, Habibie showed exceptional talent in mathematics and science. He pursued his higher education in Germany, where he studied aeronautical engineering at RWTH Aachen University. He later earned his doctorate with the highest honours and worked for the German aerospace company Messerschmitt-Bölkow-Blohm (MBB) for many years.


In 1974, President Soeharto invited Habibie to return to Indonesia to help develop the nation's technology sector. He founded PT IPTN (now PT Dirgantara Indonesia), a national aircraft manufacturing company, and successfully produced the N250 aircraft — a remarkable achievement for a developing nation.


Habibie later became Indonesia's third President in 1998, following the resignation of Soeharto. During his presidency, he introduced democratic reforms and freedom of the press. He passed away on September 11, 2019, leaving behind a legacy of innovation, courage, and national pride.
